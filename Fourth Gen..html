Fourth Generation Fighter Jets

The fourth generation of fighter jets began in the mid-1960s and continued into the early 1970s. These planes introduced significant advancements in terms of speed, maneuverability, avionics, and weaponry. They played key roles in various conflicts, including the Iraq War and others. Let's take a closer look at some of the most iconic aircraft from this era:

  • General Dynamics F-16 Fighting Falcon

    The F-16 Fighting Falcon is a multirole fighter jet that became a cornerstone of the US Air Force and numerous other air forces worldwide. Known for its agility, it is designed for air superiority, close air support, and ground attack missions. The F-16 has been used in various conflicts, including the Gulf War, Iraq War, and beyond.

    The F-15 Eagle is a twin-engine, all-weather tactical fighter designed for air superiority missions. It has been a prominent feature of the US Air Force since the 1970s, with notable success in various air combat engagements during the Gulf and Iraq Wars.
